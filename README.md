# How Energy Drives the Curve

An educational fixed income presentation exploring how energy markets, particularly crude oil, influence inflation expectations, economic growth, Treasury yields, credit conditions, liquidity, and Treasury futures markets.

## Overview

When people first enter fixed income markets, Treasury yields can feel like magic numbers moving around a screen.

In reality, Treasury yields are fundamentally driven by two variables:

- Inflation Expectations
- Real Economic Growth Expectations

Energy sits at the center of both.

Because oil influences transportation, manufacturing, industrialization, consumer spending, and credit conditions, changes in energy markets can ripple through nearly every layer of the financial system.

This presentation explores how those effects ultimately reach Treasury markets and the yield curve.

## Why This Matters

Energy is not simply another commodity.

A sufficiently large oil shock can influence:

- Treasury yields
- Treasury futures
- Credit spreads
- Corporate financing
- Consumer spending
- Venture capital funding
- Market liquidity
- Treasury market structure

Understanding these transmission pathways helps explain why rates desks, macro funds, and central banks closely monitor energy markets.

## Main Takeaway

The impact of energy extends far beyond gasoline prices.

Oil can influence inflation, growth, credit, liquidity, financing conditions, and Treasury market structure simultaneously.

Understanding Treasury markets requires understanding the real economic forces driving them, and few forces are more powerful than energy.
